The Solar Calculator
The Solar Calculator

The Solar Calculator is a passion project I developed to contribute to the renewable energy movement while showcasing software development skills. This tool allows users to input their address, energy consumption, and energy cost over the past 12 months and it will tell them how much energy and money they could have saved with solar installed at their residence. I developed this full-stack web-application from scratch and discuss it's design, logic, and stack in detail on it's Documentation page and in it's GitHub repo.

GIS Engineer & Python Developer

During my 3+ year tenure at ManTech International's Environmental Range and Sustainability Services division, I was trusted to wear many hats. Initially, I was mainly tasked with researching environmental resources and authoring technical documents regarding them. My favorite work from this time was modeling air quality emissions and authoring Air Quality resource chapters for commercial rocket launch operations - including documents for FireFly and Phantom.

About 1 year into my tenure at ManTech I was offered a position as a GIS Engineer with an emphasis on developing backend Python scripts to support my team's in-the-field efforts. I quickly proved that I had what it took to adapt to the role and lead quickly. Within a few months I was leading my team's analysis efforts, and supporting those efforts with software tools that increased report efficiency and quality. These tools helped expedite our proprietary analysis while also facilitating seamless integration between local and cloud environments.

Additionally, I was also placed on a separate team who's chief responsibility was managing 5 public facing Navy websites. This frontend centered role involved working with HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and a content management framework - Dot Net Nuke - to orchestrate the release of federal announcements and documents to the public.

Pivot

Ever since I was a child, I have had a knack for building. My grandpa was teaching me how to use scroll saws, sanders, and drills when I was in preschool. Despite my Mom not feeling comfortable with me potentially loosing a finger before I could write a sentence, my grandpa ensured me with his knowledge and trust, and helped me realize my love for building.

When I started college, I declared my major as Mechanical Engineering but became disillusioned with the path I was on after a couple years. I loved the math and science, finding a knack for these as well while setting the curve in several Physics and Calculus courses. However, as I began taking classes focused on Mechanical Engineering, I felt uninspired. I went to engineering fairs at an attempt to stoke this inspiration and all I thought was, "Do I really want to spend my career engineering springs that optimizes a mechanical component 1.5% in some arbitrary metric?"

I discussed this with classmates, friends, and academic advisors, and found myself driven towards Environmental Science, since I could continue with math and science courses while simultaneously aligning my career with something I am passionate about, the environment.

I graduated, landed my job at ManTech - an environmental consulting firm - and began my professional career. Quickly I found I preferred working on things involving, you guessed it, math and science. I gravitated towards modeling air emissions and analyzing datasets via SQL tools which was recognized by my superiors.

After about a year at ManTech, I was promoted to a GIS Engineer & Python developer. I discuss this in a bit more detail above, and also in my resume. During my 2 years in this position, I grew a strong foundation in both backend development with Python and frontend development with HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. I also gained experience with machine learning, working across cloud environments, leading analysis directives on my team, and so on. More importantly though, I found true inspiration in my work as I was working within the analytical context I grew fond of on college while also using software to do what I have loved since childhood, building.

Unfortunately, I recognized a glass ceiling in my role and this was even corroborated by my superiors. They had plenty of work for me, but the niche of the role only required so many skills and I had reached the limit; I had stagnated. I could continue writing Python scripts; or I could purse the much broader world of software development.

At this point, I was confident it was time for me to resign, find a side-job to make ends meet, and pursue a more pure form of software development. I have been self-taught since day 1 of my development career and knew I had the discipline and capacity to achieve my goal. To this day, I am developing full-stack projects, reading articles, and taking certificate courses to learn what is necessary to complete this pivot.

I am excited for this next chapter to begin at a company I am excited about. I have no doubt I will integrate into my new team well and be a teammate that others enjoy collaborating with. Through software development I have found a way to leverage my love for building in a context that I am truly inspired by. I still love building and tinkering with my hands, but building software and understanding their components has ignited a new inspiration for building. I am grateful to have found software development and can not wait to see where it takes me in my career.
