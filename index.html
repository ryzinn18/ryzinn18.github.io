Ryan in a nutshell

I am 27 years old and from San Diego, CA. Specifically, I am from Encinitas, a small beach town in Northern San Diego County. Growing up I spent most of my free time surfing and exploring San Diego with my friends. As I got older, I picked up some new hobbies, though, surfing is still my favorite of them. My new hobbies included traveling, playing music, and coding.

Currently, I live in Santa Barbara, CA. I resigned from my previous job in September '22 in favor of working at my favorite wine bar & tap house - Corks & Crowns - while I build a portfolio and train up on the skills I need to land my next job as a software developer. I still plan on working one day per week at Corks & Crowns after landing my next role though, being there feels like being at my home away from home.

The Solar Calculator
The Solar Calculator
The Solar Calculator

The Solar Calculator is a passion project I developed to contribute to the renewable energy movement while showcasing software development skills. This tool allows users to input their address, energy consumption, and energy cost over the past 12 months and it will tell them how much energy and money they could have saved with solar installed at their residence. I developed this full-stack web-application from scratch and discuss it's design, logic, and stack in detail on it's Documentation page and in it's GitHub repo .

Leather Bound Webpages
Leather Bound Webpages

Leather Bound Webpages is a personal blog I developed for my self. This was a 'two birds with one stone' opportunity where I wanted to start a blog and also wanted to develop a dynamic website using the Flask framework. I created this blog to share introspective thoughts woven into life experiences. I found myself journaling these kinds of entries and thinking, "I wish I could share these," and thus the motivation to create a blog arose. That motivation coincided with wanting to learn one of Python's web frameworks and learn how to deploy web applications. For more technical details about this project, check out it's GitHub repo .

GIS Engineer & Python Developer

During my 3+ year tenure at ManTech International's Environmental Range and Sustainability Services, I was trusted to wear many hats. Initially, I was mainly tasked with researching environmental resources and authoring technical documents regarding them. My favorite work from this time was modeling air quality emissions and authoring Air Quality resource chapters for commercial rocket launch operations - including documents for FireFly and Phantom.

About 1 year into my tenure at ManTech I was offered a position as a GIS Engineer with an emphasis on developing backend Python scripts to support my team's in-the-field efforts. I quickly proved that I had what it took to adapt to the role and lead quickly. Within a few months I was leading my team's analysis efforts, and supporting those efforts with software tools that increased report efficiency and quality. These tools helped expedite our proprietary analysis while also facilitating seamless integration between local and cloud environments.

Additionally, I was also placed on a separate team who's chief responsibility was managing 5 public facing Navy websites. This frontend centered role involved working with HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and a content management framework - Dot Net Nuke - to orchestrate the release of federal announcements and documents to the public.
